> This document outlines the expectations for participation in the CodeVerse Hub community. We are dedicated to providing a friendly, safe, and welcoming environment for everyone, regardless of experience level, gender, sexual orientation, disability, personal appearance, race, ethnicity, age, religion, or nationality.

## Our Goal

The purpose of this Code of Conduct is to set the tone for our community. It is not an exhaustive list of do's and don'ts, but rather a guide to help us all be our best selves.

We value **collaboration** and **support** just as much as technical expertise. Helping others and fostering a positive environment is highly encouraged.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by CodeVerse Hub, including:

- Our **Discord** server
- Our **GitHub** organization
- Any other platforms we operate

It applies to **all members**, including staff.

---

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- **Be kind and courteous** — Treat everyone with respect and kindness
- **Use inclusive language** — Ensure your words welcome everyone
- **Respect differing viewpoints** — We all come from different backgrounds; listen and learn from each other
- **Collaborate** — Work together and support fellow members
- **Accept criticism gracefully** — Use feedback to improve and grow
- **Prioritize the community** — Act in the best interest of CodeVerse Hub
- **Show empathy** — Understand and relate to the feelings of others

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment** — Public or private harassment of any kind
- **Inappropriate Content** — Sexualized language/imagery, or inappropriate avatars/nicknames
- **Disruptive Behavior** — Trolling, spamming, flaming, baiting, or attention-seeking
- **Attacks** — Insults, derogatory comments, or personal/political attacks
- **Doxxing** — Publishing others' private information without permission
- **Polarizing Topics** — Discussing sensitive or inflammatory subjects (politics, violence, etc.) that incite arguments
- **Unhelpful Responses** — Dismissive replies like "Just Google it" or "RTFM" to help requests

---

## Moderation Policy

CodeVerse Hub's moderation team (Moderators, Admins, Owners) enforces this Code of Conduct and our Community Rules.

1. **Enforcement** — Staff have the authority to address any behavior they deem inappropriate, even if not explicitly listed here
2. **No Public Complaints** — Do not argue about moderation actions in public channels. If you disagree with a decision, please open a ticket or DM ModMail
3. **Appeals** — You may appeal moderation decisions through the proper channels detailed below

---

## Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience a violation of this Code of Conduct, please report it immediately:

- Open a **ModMail** ticket on our Discord server
- Tag the `@Moderators` role for urgent issues (use sparingly)
- Email us at `thecodeversedev@gmail.com` for sensitive matters

> All reports are handled confidentially. We take every report seriously.

---

## Consequences of Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior will not be tolerated. Consequences may include:

- **Warning** — A formal warning about the behavior
- **Temporary Ban** — A temporary removal from the community
- **Permanent Ban** — A permanent removal from the community

---

## Appeals Process

If you believe a moderation action was unjust, you may appeal by following these steps:

1. **Open a Ticket** — Use ModMail to open a ticket explaining your case
2. **Provide Details** — Include any relevant context or evidence
3. **Wait for Review** — The moderation team will review your appeal and respond accordingly
